## 0. The strategic foundation (read this before writing anything)

**The single fact your whole site must get right:** A water filter is **not** automatically FSA/HSA eligible. It qualifies only when accompanied by a **Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN)** issued by a licensed provider, because the filter must be intended to *treat, mitigate, or prevent a specific health condition* — not just improve taste or convenience. SpringWell handles this through **TrueMed**, which runs a short health survey, has a provider issue the LMN (typically 2–5 hours), and lets you pay with an HSA/FSA card at checkout.

Why this matters for ranking:
- This is **YMYL** content (health + money). Google applies its strictest E-E-A-T scrutiny here. Sites that over-claim ("water filters are FSA eligible, period!") are factually wrong and increasingly vulnerable. The site that explains the LMN nuance accurately wins trust, featured snippets, and durable rankings.
- Accuracy *is* the conversion path. The reader's real question is "*how* do I buy one with pre-tax money?" — and the honest answer routes straight to SpringWell × TrueMed.

**Accuracy guardrails to bake into every article (your editorial style guide):**
1. Never say water filters are "automatically" or "always" FSA eligible. Always pair eligibility with **"with a Letter of Medical Necessity."**
2. State that eligibility is determined by a licensed provider and confirmed by the reader's plan administrator.
3. Contribution limits and tax brackets change yearly — write them as "current limits (verify for your plan year)" rather than hard-coding numbers that will date the page.
4. Frame savings as "up to ~30%, depending on your tax bracket," never a flat guarantee.
5. Add a short disclaimer: *not tax, legal, or medical advice; consult your plan administrator and a qualified professional.*
6. Note TrueMed is **US-only**.
7. Disclose the affiliate relationship (FTC) — this also builds trust and is an E-E-A-T positive.

**E-E-A-T signals to build sitewide (these directly affect ranking on this topic):**
- A real, named author with a bio and a stated review process; ideally a reviewer credential line ("reviewed for accuracy by…").
- An "Editorial & eligibility methodology" page explaining how you verify IRS/plan claims.
- Citations to primary sources: IRS Publication 502 and 969, TrueMed disclosures, SpringWell product/testing pages, NSF/ANSI standards, EPA contaminant pages.
- "Last updated" dates and a visible update log on the pillar.
- FTC affiliate disclosure + clear About/Contact pages.
